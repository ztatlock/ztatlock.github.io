## Goal

Reassess the homepage top summary after the CV `Overview` rewrite and decide
whether the homepage should stay as-is or receive a light authored retune.

## Decision

The homepage top summary stays structurally the same.

The slice landed only a small authored wording refresh:

- keep the homepage student-centered and public-facing
- keep the two-paragraph structure
- keep the homepage distinct from the CV `Overview`
- update the first paragraph to the approved `I pursue that goal` wording
- add one bridging sentence in the second paragraph so current research areas
  like equality saturation, numerics, and fabrication are visible

## Why This Was Enough

The homepage and CV should stay coordinated, but they should not collapse into
the same prose.

The homepage still has a different job:

- introduce the public site
- foreground student-centered mission and teaching
- stay warm and accessible

The CV `Overview` has a different job:

- summarize the evaluative record
- surface scale and recent momentum

So the right move was not a larger rewrite.
It was a small calibration so the two surfaces no longer feel out of phase.

## Result

After this slice:

- the homepage top summary still reads like a homepage
- the CV `Overview` still reads like an executive summary
- the two surfaces now share a more current research vocabulary without
  becoming redundant
